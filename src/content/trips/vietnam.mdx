## Overview

Route: Ho Chi Minh City, Hue, Hoi An, Ha Long, Sapa, Hanoi.

A run up the length of the country. Fly into Saigon (HCMC) in the south, work north by short domestic flights and a couple of overnight transfers, and fly out of Hanoi. Twelve days covers the headline stops: the Mekong and Cu Chi in the south, the imperial city of Hue and the tailor town of Hoi An in the centre, then Ha Long Bay, the mountains around Sapa, and Hanoi in the north.

*Book an open-jaw (multi-city) ticket: in to Saigon and out of Hanoi, or the reverse. A round-trip into one city means doubling back the length of the country for no reason.*

This trip ran late December into early January. The south was dry and warm, which is its best season. The catch is the north: Sapa, Fansipan, and Ha Long are cold and often misty in midwinter, and central Vietnam (Hue, Hoi An) is on the tail of its rainy season. For the whole country to line up at once, February to April is the safer window.

### Getting around

Domestic flights (Vietnam Airlines, VietJet, Bamboo) cover the long legs cheaply, Saigon to Hue and Da Nang to Hai Phong here. Between nearby towns, sleeper buses and limousine vans are the norm, including the overnight legs to Sapa and back to Hanoi. Within cities, the ride apps Grab and Xanh SM (the Vingroup electric-taxi app, now branded Green SM) are everywhere and cheaper than flagging taxis.

### SIM and money

Sort the SIM and an initial currency exchange at the airport, but compare the counters first, both SIM prices and exchange rates vary between them. For larger exchanges later, the jewellery shops in town often give the best rates; check the Google reviews before picking one. Currency is the dong (VND) and the notes carry a lot of zeros, so watch the count. Keep cash for tours, buses, and markets; Grab and the bigger places take cards. A Viettel or Mobifone SIM works countrywide, or load an eSIM before you fly.

### Coffee

Do not leave without working through the coffee. Vietnam runs on it, and it goes well beyond the standard ca phe sua da (iced coffee with condensed milk):

- Salt coffee (ca phe muoi), a Hue specialty
- Egg coffee (ca phe trung), the Hanoi classic, with a custardy whipped-egg top
- Coconut coffee, blended with coconut cream
- Even the Vietnamese Starbucks runs a local menu worth a look

### Veg food

Easier than you would expect. Vegan and vegetarian options are everywhere once you ask: banh mi (skip the pate and meat), fresh rolls with tofu and veg, rice-and-vegetable plates, and veggie burgers. **Pizza 4P's** (a homegrown chain) is excellent and all over the country. Look for com chay (Buddhist vegetarian) spots too. The one thing to watch is fish sauce (nuoc mam), it goes into almost everything by default, so ask them to leave it out and confirm before ordering.

## Day 1, Ho Chi Minh City

Land in Saigon (HCMC), drop bags at the hotel, and spend the day on the city centre on foot. An early arrival gives you the full day here.

- Ben Thanh Market for the first wander and lunch
- War Remnants Museum, sobering but worth it
- Notre-Dame Cathedral and the Central Post Office
- Reunification Palace
- Bui Vien walking street after dark for the night-market buzz

Book the next day's Cu Chi and Mekong tour tonight if you have not already.

## Day 2, Cu Chi Tunnels and the Mekong Delta

A long combo day trip: the **Cu Chi Tunnels** northwest of the city in the morning (the Viet Cong tunnel network, with a crawl-through section), then south to the **Mekong Delta** for boats through the canals, a coconut-candy workshop, and river life. *The two sit on opposite sides of Saigon, so this is a packed twelve-hour day. If you would rather not rush, split them across two days or pick one; Cu Chi is the shorter half-day.*

## Day 3, Hue

Morning flight from Saigon to Hue, check in, and spend the afternoon on the old imperial capital.

- Imperial Citadel and the Forbidden Purple City
- Thien Mu Pagoda on the Perfume River
- The royal tombs (Tu Duc and Khai Dinh are the standouts) if time allows

Try salt coffee here; it is a Hue invention. **Mingle Vegetarian Cafe & Restaurant** is a reliable veg stop.

## Day 4, Hue to Hoi An

Travel to Hoi An over the **Hai Van Pass**, the coastal mountain road is the scenic way to do this leg. A private cab is very affordable, especially split between a group of four, and nicer than the bus; it also lets you stop at the Marble Mountains near Da Nang on the way.

Get to Hoi An by afternoon and **order tailored clothes first thing**: the shops need a day or two for fittings, so ordering now means you collect before you leave. *FYI: prices are roughly in line with tailoring in India, so it may not feel as cheap as the hype suggests; go for the quick turnaround and the experience rather than a bargain.* Then the evening is for the **Ancient Town**, the lantern-lit UNESCO old quarter, the Japanese Covered Bridge, and the river with its floating candles.

**Kurumi** is a good vegetarian and vegan spot in town, and **XLIII Coffee** is a lovely specialty coffee shop worth a stop for the brews.

## Day 5, Da Nang day trip

Day trip to Da Nang from Hoi An (about 45 minutes by cab; I found basing in Hoi An nicer than staying in Da Nang).

- Marble Mountains, caves and pagodas with viewpoints
- My Khe Beach
- Son Tra peninsula and the Lady Buddha statue
- **Dragon Bridge fire-and-water show** in the evening: it breathes fire and sprays water on Fri/Sat/Sun nights at 9 PM (free), then drive back to Hoi An

**Chickpea Eatery** in Da Nang is a good vegetarian stop for lunch.

**Optional add-on:** Ba Na Hills and the **Golden Bridge** (the giant stone hands), reached by cable car above Da Nang. I skipped it, but if it is a must-do, go early to beat the crowds and consider a night in Da Nang to fit it in.

## Day 6, Hoi An to Ha Long

Collect your tailored clothes in the morning, then fly from Da Nang to Hai Phong and transfer to Ha Long (about 1.5 hours by road). Check in and rest.

*The cruise is the must-do here, but Ha Long town itself does not have much to do. If I did it again I would skip staying in Ha Long and cruise from a quieter base instead: Cat Ba island and the Lan Ha Bay next door have the same karst scenery with far fewer boats, and you can book it with a transfer out of Hanoi. The day-by-day below is how I actually travelled it.*

## Day 7, Ha Long Bay

Full-day cruise through the limestone karsts: a cave stop, kayaking, and lunch on the boat. This is the highlight of the north, so do not skip the cruise even if you trim elsewhere. Cruising the quieter **Lan Ha Bay** out of Cat Ba (see the note above) gets you the same scenery with fewer boats. Back at the pier by late afternoon, then the long overnight transfer to Sapa. It routes back through Hanoi, so it is a haul; an overnight bus is the usual way.

**Overnight cruises** are the other option, with a night spent on the boat in the bay. They cost a fair bit more and the experience felt much the same to me, so I stuck with the day cruise; worth it only if sleeping out on the water is the draw for you.

**Nha Hang Hoi Chay** is a good vegan restaurant in Ha Long if you need a meal off the boat.

## Day 8, Sapa and Fansipan

Arrive in Sapa, the hill town in the far north terraced with rice paddies. Head up **Fansipan**, the highest peak in Indochina, by the Sun World cable car (then a short funicular or the steps to the summit). Dress warm: it is cold and often in cloud in midwinter, but you climb above it.

## Day 9, Cat Cat village, on to Hanoi

Walk down to **Cat Cat village**, the Hmong village below Sapa, an easy loop past terraced fields and waterfalls. Back up for the evening, then the overnight transfer to Hanoi.

## Day 10, Hanoi and the incense village

Arrive in Hanoi in the morning. Day trip out to the **incense village** (Quang Phu Cau, about an hour south), where bundles of red incense sticks are fanned out to dry, the photogenic stop. Back in the city for the evening: the Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem Lake, and your first egg coffee, a Hanoi original.

## Day 11, Ninh Binh day trip

Full-day trip to **Ninh Binh** (about 2 hours south), the "Ha Long Bay on land": limestone peaks over rice fields and rivers.

- Trang An or Tam Coc boat ride through the caves and karsts
- Mua Cave for the climb (about 500 steps) to the panoramic viewpoint
- Hoa Lu, the ancient capital, or Bai Dinh pagoda if you have time

Back in Hanoi for the **weekend night market** (Hang Dao to Dong Xuan, Fri to Sun evenings).

## Day 12, Hanoi and flight out

Last day for shopping and anything you missed in the city: the Old Quarter for souvenirs, Train Street, the Temple of Literature, or the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum complex. Then to the airport for the flight home.
