## Overview

Route: New York (with a Niagara Falls day trip), Philadelphia, Washington DC (with a Virginia countryside day). New York to Philadelphia by Amtrak, Philadelphia to DC by bus.

New York eats most of the trip, and rightly so. Pace assumes a lot of walking, the subway for longer hops, and one full day written off to the Niagara bus.

Fall is a good window: the weather is mild and the foliage on the Niagara run is worth the early start.

### Book these before anything else

- **9/11 Memorial Museum (NYC).** Only the indoor museum needs a timed ticket; the outdoor memorial pools are free and open. Book the museum a few days out.
- **One NYC observation deck.** Pick one (Empire State Building, Top of the Rock, or SUMMIT One Vanderbilt) and book a timed/skip-line ticket to avoid long queues.
- **The FRIENDS Experience (NYC).** Timed slots, sells out on weekends.
- **The Met (NYC).** Timed entry; it is enormous, give it half a day.
- **Niagara Falls day trip (from NYC).** I booked a package with return bus and falls tickets; reserve a few days ahead.
- **US Capitol tour (DC).** Free, but book the timed tour in advance.
- **Broadway show (NYC).** I tried the digital lottery and rush via TodayTix for discounted seats and didn't win; full price is steep, so decide early if you want a guaranteed seat.
- **Bungalow by Vikas Khanna (NYC).** I tried to get a table and couldn't for weeks. Reserve far ahead if you want it.

### Vegetarian food + drink

- **New York-style pizza.** Non-negotiable. A plain cheese slice, folded, from a corner pizzeria.
- **Bagels.** A toasted bagel with cream cheese is the classic NYC breakfast.
- **Levain Bakery cookie.** Thick, gooey, worth the hype. I had mine in Georgetown, DC; there are NYC branches too.
- **Georgetown Cupcake (DC).** The Georgetown institution.
- **Brewery food (Virginia).** The breweries out in Loudoun County have food trucks and kitchens with veg options; the draw is the setting and the beer.

### SIM and data

Buy a US eSIM before you fly so you have data the moment you land (handy for the JFK cab and maps). Browse providers and pick a plan that matches your trip length and data needs; install the QR on the phone you'll actually carry, since these are single-use, and activate after you land so the validity clock starts then.

### Cards

I paid with a Scapia card throughout for the zero forex markup. Carry a little cash too, since some smaller spots and tips run easier on cash. More on the cards I travel with in [travel tips](/wanderlust/tips#cards).

## Day 1, Arrival in New York

Landed at JFK. Immigration queues are long, so budget a couple of hours after you land.

I booked an Uber straight to my stay. Rides from JFK into Manhattan are expensive (more so with surge), but with two suitcases and a backpack it wasn't really a choice.

- **Cheaper alt:** if you're travelling light, the AirTrain to the subway gets you into Manhattan for a fraction of the cab fare.
- Evening, once settled: pick any one observation deck for skyline views (Empire State Building, Top of the Rock, or SUMMIT One Vanderbilt; *book ahead*) and walk around the area.

## Day 2, Lower Manhattan and Brooklyn

A walking day through downtown and over the bridge.

- Financial District
- 9/11 Memorial and the World Trade Center / Oculus (the outdoor memorial is free; *book the museum ahead* only if you want to go inside)
- Walk across the Brooklyn Bridge into DUMBO
- The classic Manhattan Bridge shot from Washington Street in DUMBO
- Chinatown for dinner

**Statue of Liberty:** two ways to do it. The free Staten Island Ferry from the southern tip of Manhattan passes close enough for a distant view and photos, with no ticket needed, and it's quick. To actually set foot on Liberty Island it's a separate ticketed ferry from Battery Park, and between the queues and security you should set aside the better part of a day. Pick based on how much time you want to give it.

## Day 3, Midtown

The big midtown loop, mostly on foot.

- High Line walk
- Vessel at Hudson Yards (free to see from the plaza; a timed ticket is only needed to go up inside)
- Rockefeller area
- St Patrick's Cathedral
- Grand Central Terminal
- New York Public Library (the main branch with the stone lions)
- Madison Square
- The FRIENDS Experience near Flatiron *(book ahead)*
- Times Square
- Grab a proper New York-style pizza slice somewhere along the way

## Day 4, Niagara Falls day trip

A long day, early start. I booked a package with return bus and falls tickets, so I didn't have to sort transit or entry myself. The bus each way is several hours.

We kept to the **US side** of the falls. The Canadian side has the better head-on view but needs a separate Canadian visa, so plan that in advance if you want it.

## Day 5, Central Park, the Met, Roosevelt Island

Uptown and the park.

- The Met *(timed entry; give it half a day)*
- Central Park. I spent a lot of time here and caught a concert in the park.
- Roosevelt Island via the aerial cable car (Roosevelt Island Tramway). It runs on a normal metro swipe, and the ride over the East River is a great view.
- Times Square at night, to see it fully lit

## Day 6, New York to Philadelphia

Amtrak from New York Penn Station to Philadelphia 30th Street, around 1.5 hours.

- Philadelphia City Hall
- Reading Terminal Market
- Wander the historic centre
- Overnight in Philadelphia

## Day 7, Philadelphia to Washington DC

Bus from Philadelphia to DC, around 3 hours.

- **Bag tip:** I paid $20 extra for a second bag. If you're over the one-bag allowance, it's worth asking fellow passengers on the same bus who are travelling light whether you can put a bag under their free allowance.
- A friend picked me up and sorted a university transit card, so local metro and bus rides were free. If you have a local or student contact, ask.
- National Mall in the evening: Lincoln Memorial, Washington Monument, and the war memorials. They look great lit up at night.

## Day 8, DC monuments and museums

The Smithsonian core, all walkable on and around the Mall.

- National Air and Space Museum (free)
- National Gallery of Art Sculpture Garden
- US Capitol *(free timed tour, book ahead)*
- Library of Congress
- White House from the outside. Tours were closed during my October 2024 visit because of the upcoming US election, so we only saw it from the fence. When they do run, tours need an advance request well ahead of time.

## Day 9, Georgetown

Give this a full day. Georgetown is one of the prettiest parts of DC, and easily my favourite, so don't rush it. Even the bus ride over is part of the charm.

Spend the day wandering: the historic cobblestone streets, the shops and cafes along M Street, the C&O Canal towpath, and the Georgetown waterfront on the Potomac. We rode electric scooters around, which is a great way to cover it.

Eat your way through it too:

- A cookie from Levain Bakery
- Cupcakes from Georgetown Cupcake

In the evening, head to Old Town Alexandria across the river. A charming, walkable waterfront old town, very nice for dinner and a stroll.

## Day 10, Virginia countryside

Rented a car with friends and drove out into the Virginia countryside (Loudoun County), rolling hills and a good day out of the city.

- Harvest Gap Brewery
- Bear Chase Brewing

## Day 11, Travel out

Fly home from Dulles (IAD) or Reagan National (DCA), depending on your route.
