## Overview

Route: Phi Phi, Krabi, Koh Samui, Phuket.

An island loop linked entirely by ferries. You fly into Phuket but head straight out to Phi Phi on arrival and work your way back, ending in Phuket for the flight home. Phi Phi, Krabi, and Phuket sit on the Andaman coast; Koh Samui is over on the Gulf side, which is the one longer transfer.

Late March worked for the whole route, but the two coasts run on different seasons. The Andaman side (Phi Phi, Krabi, Phuket) is driest November to April. The Gulf side (Koh Samui) is the opposite, wettest around October to December and best from January, so the window where both line up is roughly January to March.

### Getting around

Every leg was a ferry, each around 1,000 to 2,000 INR per person. The Phi Phi, Krabi, and Phuket hops are short. Koh Samui is the long one: you cross from the Andaman side overland to Donsak pier and ferry across to the Gulf, so budget most of a day for it.

### SIM and money

I picked up a SIM at the airport on arrival, and had exchanged currency back in India beforehand. For spending I carried a BookMyForex prepaid forex card; more on the cards I travel with in [travel tips](/wanderlust/tips#cards). Carry some US dollars too, since immigration can ask for proof of funds (see [visa notes](/wanderlust/visa#thailand)).

### Vegetarian food

There are plenty of Indian restaurants, so you'll never be stuck. Outside those, vegetarian options thin out, so I carried some [ready-to-eat meals](/wanderlust/tips#food) as backup. Fruit, bread, and drinks are easy to find everywhere. At restaurants, confirm the cooking oil before you order, since many places cook in fish oil.

## Day 1, Arrival and Phi Phi

Fly into Phuket (HKT), land around midday, and head straight to the pier for the ferry to Phi Phi. Check in by evening.

Book the next day's island tour as soon as you arrive, and your scuba slot too if you can. Then ease into Phi Phi's nightlife: the beaches run fire shows after dark, which are worth catching.

## Day 2, Phi Phi

Full island-tour day. The boat trips loop the bays for snorkelling and beach stops, with **Maya Bay** (the cove from the film *The Beach*) the headline. One of our tours also included a night stop for bioluminescent plankton, worth seeing, though fainter than the Maldives. Back for the evening, with more nightlife and fire shows if you have the energy.

## Day 3, Phi Phi to Krabi

Scuba in the morning, the reefs around Phi Phi are some of the best on this stretch. Evening ferry to Krabi, check in, and rest. Sort out the next day's plan and book an island tour.

## Day 4, Krabi

Krabi's draw is its limestone cliffs, caves, and beaches.

- Railay Beach, reachable only by longtail boat
- Phra Nang cave beach, with the shrine cave at one end
- Island-hopping tour (the 4 or 7 island tours are the common ones)
- Ao Nang for the evening, walk the strip and the night market

**Alt:** swap the island day for an inland one. The Emerald Pool (a clear natural pool in the jungle) and the nearby hot springs are about an hour from Ao Nang.

## Day 5, Krabi to Koh Samui

Travel to Donsak pier and ferry across to Koh Samui. Check in, then rent a scooter to roam the island for the afternoon. The land sights are an easy loop:

- Wat Plai Laem, a Buddhist temple
- Hin Ta Hin Ya, the Grandfather and Grandmother rocks
- Na Muang waterfall
- Secret Buddha Garden, up in the hills

In the evening, head to **Chaweng beach** for a drag show. Paris Follies (Ladyboy Cabaret) is the well-known one.

## Day 6, Koh Samui

Full-day **Ang Thong National Marine Park** tour: a cluster of islands with viewpoints, a lagoon, snorkelling, and kayaking. Government-approved boat tours run all-inclusive packages, around 2 to 5k INR per person, or roughly 10k with snorkelling and kayaking added.

Catch a beach fire show in the evening, then an early night before the early ferry out.

## Day 7, Koh Samui to Phuket

Early ferry back to the Andaman side and across to Phuket. Spend the day on Phuket's sights:

- Big Buddha, the white marble statue on the hill
- Wat Chalong, the island's most important temple
- Old Town Phuket, the Sino-Portuguese streets. I stopped at **Hog's Head**, a Harry Potter-themed cafe, for pizza and drinks.
- **Tiger Kingdom** is on the way, but I didn't go in. The welfare side of tiger-petting attractions didn't sit right, and it's an easy skip.
- Bangla Road in Patong once it gets dark, for the nightlife

## Day 8, Phuket

A slower day for the beaches and anything you missed.

- Beach day: Freedom Beach, or Karon and Kamala
- Patong if you want it busier
- Chillva Market in Phuket Town for an evening night-market wander

**Alt:** trade the beach day for a boat tour to **Phang Nga Bay** (the James Bond Island limestone stacks) or the **Similan Islands** (further out, known for clear water and snorkelling).

## Day 9, Travel out

Transfer to Phuket airport in time for the flight home. You're already on Phuket by now, so it's a road transfer, no ferry.
