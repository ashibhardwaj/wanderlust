## Overview

Route: Málaga, Granada (day trip), Seville, Barcelona, Valencia, Buñol (Tomatina), Mallorca *or* Ibiza, Madrid, Porto, Lisbon.

Best window is the last full week of August, anchored on Tomatina (always the last Wednesday of August). Pace assumes 2 nights in most cities and 1 to 2 long travel days.

### Book these before anything else

- **Alhambra (Granada).** 2 to 3 months out, sells out hard.
- **Sagrada Família + Park Güell (Barcelona).** A few weeks out, timed slots.
- **Tomatina (Buñol).** Paid ticket required, book by July, capped at ~20,000 people.
- **Real Alcázar (Seville).** A few days out, skips a long line.

### Vegetarian food + drink

- **Gazpacho** (cold tomato soup). Best in Andalucía, so order it across Málaga and Seville.
- **Vegan paella.** Try it in Valencia, where paella was born.
- **Patatas bravas.** Standard tapas-bar order, especially in Madrid and Barcelona.
- **Churros con chocolate.** Madrid is the spiritual home, but you'll find them everywhere.
- **Sangria, vermouth on tap, aperol spritz.** All afternoon drinks, try whichever the local bar pushes.
- **Port wine.** Only at a Vila Nova de Gaia cellar in Porto. That's the rule.
- **Pastel de nata.** Pastéis de Belém in Lisbon is the original. Try one from a neighbourhood bakery too, for comparison.

## Day 1, Málaga

Arrival day. The old town is compact, everything below is walking distance.

- Atarazanas Market (closes mid-afternoon, hit first)
- Lunch stop: gazpacho at any old-town cafe
- Alcazaba Palace
- Picasso Museum
- Málaga Cathedral
- Centre Pompidou Málaga
- Sunset at Malagueta Beach

## Day 2, Granada day trip

Earliest Málaga to Granada bus is around 6:45 AM, two hours each way. Last bus back is 9 PM.

- Alhambra: Nasrid Palaces + Generalife *(book ahead)*
- Lunch in Albaicín
- Granada centre walk
- Bus back to Málaga

**Alt:** book a full-day Málaga-to-Granada tour via GetYourGuide if you don't want to deal with transit yourself.

## Day 3, Málaga to Seville

Train Málaga to Seville is about 2 hours. Check in, then walk:

- Real Alcázar. Includes the Mudéjar Palace, which is the Water Gardens of Dorne in Game of Thrones. *(book ahead)*
- Seville Cathedral + La Giralda
- Torre del Oro
- Cross the Triana Bridge, walk through Triana Market
- Sangria break at a patio bar in Santa Cruz
- Dinner in Santa Cruz, or Alameda de Hércules for nightlife
- Optional: late-evening flamenco show

## Day 4, Skydive + Seville to Barcelona

Big day. Skydive early, easy afternoon, evening train out.

- Skydive Spain in Bollullos, ~30 min from Seville. Slots are 7-8 AM. This is the drop zone from *Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara*. Walk-ins worked for me, no need to book ahead.
  - **Discount tip:** ask the name of someone you know who jumped there recently. A friend-of-a-friend works too. They have a referral discount.
  - **Transport:** Uber from Seville works. If you're staying at a hostel, post in the common-room chat the night before and split the ride. Jumpers are usually on the same early slot.
- Brunch back in town
- Metropol Parasol (Las Setas)
- Plaza de España, late afternoon, when the light is best
- Evening AVE train to Barcelona, around 5.5 hours. No direct overnight train on this route. FlixBus has overnight options if you want to skip a hotel night.

## Day 5, Barcelona, day 1

Gaudí + Gothic. Both biggies need pre-booked tickets.

- Park Güell, morning slot *(book ahead)*
- La Sagrada Família, lunch-ish slot *(book ahead)*
- Metro to the Gothic Quarter, wander
- Barcelona Cathedral
- Patatas bravas at any tapas bar
- Dinner in El Born

## Day 6, Barcelona, day 2

Slower second day. More neighbourhood, less monument.

- Casa Batlló or Casa Milà (pick one)
- La Boqueria market off La Rambla
- Barceloneta beach for the afternoon, with an aperol spritz at a chiringuito
- Sunset at Bunkers del Carmel or the Montjuïc cable car
- Nightlife: Barcelona reportedly does Ibiza-grade clubs for less

**Alt:** swap most of Day 6 for a Montserrat day trip. About an hour by train from Plaça d'Espanya, then the rack railway up to the Benedictine monastery. Short hikes around the rock formations and a viewpoint over Catalonia are the draws. Worth it if you've had your fill of city.

## Day 7, Barcelona to Valencia

Train is about 3 hours. Check in, explore the old town.

- Valencia Cathedral (holds the Santo Cáliz, claimed Holy Grail relic)
- Plaza de la Virgen + City Hall
- La Lonja de la Seda *(UNESCO)*
- Iglesia de San Nicolás
- Mercado Central (closes mid-afternoon)
- Lunch: vegan paella at a rice-focused spot, this is paella's hometown
- Optional: bus to the City of Arts and Sciences for the architecture
- Dinner and sunset at Valencia Marina

## Day 8, Tomatina

Bus Valencia to Buñol around 8-9 AM. Festival runs noon to 2 PM.

- Tomatina *(book ahead, sells out fast)*
  - **What I paid:** €50 pp package including round-trip bus from Valencia. T-shirt was an extra €10.
  - **Carry nothing.** There aren't really washrooms to change, and anything you bring gets tomato'd. Just your phone, plus some cash or a card for food and drinks. Wear clothes and shoes you can throw out.
- Back to Valencia for showers
- Optional: Tomatina after-party in Valencia (~€20 with a drink)
- Or chill at the marina + flamenco show

## Day 9, Valencia to Mallorca *or* Ibiza

Short flight from Valencia. **Pick one based on vibe.** This itinerary doesn't do both.

### Mallorca (chill, what I did)

Base in Palma. Beach day at Cala Mondragó or Es Trenc, evenings in Palma's old town. A scooter or a rental car helps a lot for the beaches.

### Ibiza (party)

Base near Playa d'en Bossa or San Antonio. Beach by day, clubs by night. Snorkelling boats to Es Vedrà are worth booking.

## Day 10, Mallorca or Ibiza, day 2

### Mallorca

Day trip to Sóller or Valldemossa. The train through the Tramuntana mountains is the point. Or hit Es Trenc if you didn't yesterday.

### Ibiza

Second beach, a boat trip, and another club. Or ferry to Formentera (quieter, turquoise water).

## Day 11, To Madrid

Morning flight Palma or Ibiza to Madrid. Check in, walk the centre.

- Puerta del Sol
- Plaza Mayor
- Nun cookies at Monasterio del Corpus Christi. You order through a turnstile and never see the nuns.
- Royal Palace of Madrid
- Mercado San Miguel (touristy but fine for a tapas crawl)
- Churros con chocolate at Chocolatería San Ginés (since 1894, the iconic one)

All walking distance.

## Day 12, Madrid full day

Pick one of two paths.

### Museum day

One of Prado, Reina Sofía, or Thyssen-Bornemisza. Prado for old masters, Reina Sofía for *Guernica*, Thyssen for breadth. Add Crystal Palace in Retiro Park, Gran Vía at dusk, tapas in La Latina.

### Toledo day trip

30 min by AVE. The whole walled city is UNESCO. Pair with a shorter Madrid evening (Prado is free for the last 2 hours most days).

Overnight FlixBus to Porto departs Madrid late evening, around 9 hours. **What I did.** Saves a hotel night and a flight. Alt: sleep in Madrid and fly out next morning, ~1.5 hours.

## Day 13, Porto

Arrive morning from the overnight bus. Compact city; most of this is walking distance.

- Livraria Lello (the bookstore associated with Harry Potter lore; ticketed entry)
- Porto Cathedral
- Palácio da Bolsa
- Church of Saint Ildefonso + Chapel of Souls (blue *azulejo* tiles, very Instagram)
- São Bento railway station (more tiles)
- Cross the Dom Luís I bridge to Vila Nova de Gaia for port wine tasting at a riverside cellar. Graham's, Taylor's, and Sandeman are the famous ones.

## Day 14, Porto to Lisbon

Train Porto to Lisbon is 2.5 to 3.5 hours. Arrive afternoon, light evening.

- Praça do Comércio
- Rossio Square + Rossio Station
- Santa Justa Lift. Look, don't queue. The view from Miradouro de Santa Justa above it is the same for free.
- Dinner in Bairro Alto

## Day 15, Lisbon, full day

Two loops.

### Morning, Belém *(UNESCO area)*

- Jerónimos Monastery
- Belém Tower
- Pastéis de Belém (the original; the queue moves fast). Try a pastel de nata from a neighbourhood bakery too, for comparison.

### Afternoon and evening, Alfama

- Lisbon Cathedral
- Church of São Roque. Most ornate baroque interior in the city, often more recommended than the cathedral.
- Graça Church
- Miradouro da Senhora do Monte for sunset (highest viewpoint in Lisbon)

**Alt:** swap this for a full Sintra day (Pena Palace, Quinta da Regaleira). Don't try to do Lisbon and Sintra in one day.

## Day 16, Travel out

Lisbon airport is close to the centre, about an hour door to gate.
