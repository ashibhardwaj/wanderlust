## Overview

Route: Kandy, Nuwara Eliya (en route), Ella, Mirissa, Galle, Colombo.

A loop starting and ending in Colombo: hill country first, then drop south to the coast and return to Colombo for the flight out. The scenic train routes are a highlight, especially the hill-country line up to Ella and the coastal line from Galle back to Colombo.

December and January are the dry season for the south and west. Good whale watching window off Mirissa too.

### Trains

The scenic routes are the ones to plan around: the hill-country line up to Ella is the famous stretch, and the coastal line from Galle to Colombo is lovely too. Reserve your seats in advance. It's cheap, and worth going for first or a reserved class, but trains and seats are limited and the popular routes sell out fast, so book early.

### Money

Exchange dollars at the airport. Rates there were on par with the city, and not every town had reliable exchange counters. Carry some cash for trains, tuk-tuks, and smaller cafes. Packaged food and everyday goods cost noticeably more than in India, so it's worth stocking up before you fly.

### Veg food

Not a problem anywhere on this route. Sri Lankan cuisine is naturally vegetarian-friendly - rice and curry, kottu, hoppers, pol roti. You will not need to hunt for options.

## Day 1, Kandy

Uber or cab from the airport to Colombo Fort station, then train to Kandy (~3 to 3.5 hours). Compact city, most things are walkable or a short tuk-tuk ride.

- Sri Dalada Maligawa (Temple of the Tooth Relic)
- Kandy Lake, walk the perimeter at dusk
- Bahiravokanda Vihara (the large white Buddha visible from the city)
- Peradeniya Royal Botanical Gardens
- Evening Kandyan cultural show if you want the traditional dance

**Optional add-on (requires an extra day):** Sigiriya is around 3 hours north of Kandy. The Lion Rock fortress is worth it if you have time - either stop there before coming to Kandy, or add a day here. Dambulla Cave Temple is on the same road.

### Skip

**Cafe Nuwara** - racist staff, bad service.

## Day 2, Nuwara Eliya + Ella

Cab from Kandy to Ella, stopping in Nuwara Eliya for the afternoon. Around 3 hours total driving.

Nuwara Eliya stops:
- Pedro or Mackwoods Tea Estate - factory tour, tasting
- Gregory Lake
- The town itself has a colonial "Little England" feel, worth a short walk

Continue to Ella by evening. Settle in, walk down Ella town.

## Day 3, Ella

- Nine Arches Bridge - 20 minute walk from town, best in the morning before the tour buses arrive
- Ravana Falls on the road from Nuwara Eliya side
- Little Adam's Peak - easy 2-hour hike, good panoramic views, recommended
- Ella Rock - harder 4-hour hike, better views, worth it if you have the legs for it
- Tea estate walks wind through the plantations around town

**Kiri Kopi** is the go-to coffee spot in Ella.

**Optional add-on:** Yala National Park fits between Ella and Mirissa if you add a day. Morning jeep safari, then continue to Mirissa in the afternoon.

## Day 4, Mirissa

Head down from Ella to Mirissa on the coast, around 3 to 4 hours. Check in, slow afternoon.

- Coconut Tree Hill
- Parrot Rock
- Mirissa Beach

**Dhana's Curry Pot** - fixed menu, unlimited rice and curry. Good value, fills you up.

**La Mer** - good option for a sit-down meal.

## Day 5, Mirissa

- Whale watching - January is within the season for blue and sperm whales off Mirissa, book a morning boat
- Explore beaches nearby

## Day 6, Galle

Move to Galle in the morning, around 45 minutes from Mirissa. The fort is best in the late afternoon and evening when the day-trip crowds thin out.

- Galle Fort (UNESCO) - walk the ramparts
- Dutch Reformed Church
- National Maritime Museum
- Galle Lighthouse at sunset
- Dinner inside the fort walls

Staying the night is worth it for the early morning before the buses arrive and the atmosphere after dark.

## Day 7, Colombo

Coastal train from Galle to Colombo, around 2 to 2.5 hours. The sea-facing stretch is one of the prettiest train rides in the country. Last day before the flight - good for the city.

- Marino Beach Colombo runs a free city tour from 2:30 to 6:30 PM, covers the main landmarks
- Pettah Market for chaotic, cheap shopping
- Colombo 7 (Cinnamon Gardens) for a quieter neighbourhood walk
- Galle Face Green for the evening sea breeze

**Seed Cafe** is worth a stop for coffee and a sit-down. Service is slow so don't go if you're short on time.

## Day 8, Return

Colombo airport is close to the city, straightforward to reach from most hotels.
