## Overview

Route: Sharm el-Sheikh, Cairo, Alexandria or Baharia Desert (day trip), Luxor, Aswan + Abu Simbel (day trip), Hurghada, Cairo.

A loop anchored on Cairo. Red Sea diving first, then the historical core down the Nile, with the long legs covered overnight. I went in late August, which is off-season - hot, but cheaper and quieter. The Red Sea resorts are built around the heat and the historical sites are fine with early starts. October to April is the comfortable window if you'd rather skip it.

### Getting around

All inter-city travel on this trip was by bus, mainly to save cost - Egypt's long-distance bus network is solid and far cheaper than flying. The overnight legs double as hotel nights, which helps the budget further.

### SIM and money

The same travel agent who arranged my visa guarantee letter also did an airport meet-and-greet and sorted both of these on the spot. He set me up with a local Vodafone SIM (around 200 INR) and exchanged my US dollars at the Google rate, no markup. I carried dollars in for that reason. Worth asking your agent whether they do the same.

### Vegetarian food

Egyptian street food is very veg-friendly. What I ate and would order again:

- Koshari, the national dish and entirely vegetarian: rice, lentils, macaroni, and chickpeas under a spiced tomato sauce with fried onions
- Ful medames, the breakfast staple: fava beans slow-cooked and mashed with oil, lemon, and cumin, eaten with bread
- Mesa'ah, eggplant cooked down in tomato sauce
- Ta'meya (Egyptian falafel, made from fava beans) and veg shawarma wraps
- Om Ali, a warm bread pudding with milk and nuts, for dessert

## Day 1, Arrival

Land and head straight to the Cairo bus terminal after immigration, for the overnight bus to Sharm el-Sheikh. The drive is around 5 to 6 hours and gets you in early morning.

If your flight lands early in the day, there's time to eat out and roam around Cairo before catching the bus. Stash your bags first so you're not dragging them around. The airport itself has no reliable baggage storage, but apps like Bounce or Radical Storage have luggage-storage spots around downtown Cairo.

## Day 2, Sharm el-Sheikh

Arrive early morning. Check in and sleep.

Rest of the day is slow: beach, the Naama Bay strip if you want a walk, dinner by the water.

## Day 3, Sharm el-Sheikh

Scuba in the morning. The reefs around Sharm, including Ras Mohammed National Park, are some of the best in the Red Sea. Most dive centres on Naama Bay run morning boats, around 3,500 INR per dive (photos and video extra). Book the day before.

Overnight bus to Cairo in the evening.

## Day 4, Cairo

Arrive morning. Check in, then head to Giza.

- Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx
- Solar Boat Museum (the reconstructed funerary boat of Khufu, next to the Great Pyramid)
- Optional: camel ride along the plateau for the full-pyramid-row view

The site is large. Go early for the light and to beat the crowds.

## Day 5, Cairo

Full day out of the city - two options depending on what you booked.

### Alexandria day trip

I did it as a booked day-trip package with a private cab, about 2.5 hours each way. A coastal city, Greek and Roman past visible in the archaeology.

- Bibliotheca Alexandrina
- Qaitbay Citadel on the waterfront
- Catacombs of Kom el Shoqafa
- Lunch along the Corniche

### Baharia Desert tour

Organised day trip from Cairo, usually a full-day jeep route.

- White Desert - chalk formations in surreal shapes
- Black Desert - volcanic hills, dark rock scattered across the sand
- Crystal Mountain
- Hot springs stop

Overnight bus to Luxor from Cairo.

## Day 6, Luxor

Arrive morning. Check in.

Aswan day trip from Luxor. I didn't make it to Aswan, but if you can, try to get on a tour that includes Abu Simbel - it is the most recommended thing I didn't do on this trip.

Going south from Luxor, the order is:

- Edfu (Temple of Horus)
- Kom Ombo (Temple of Sobek and Haroeris)
- Aswan: Nubian villages on the banks of the Nile - the houses are painted in vivid blues and yellows with Nubian murals and artwork. Distinct culture from the rest of Egypt, worth time on its own.
- Abu Simbel: another 3 hours south of Aswan, near the Sudan border. Two temples carved into a cliff face for Ramesses II, physically relocated in the 1960s to save them from Lake Nasser. Most tours do an early morning drive from Aswan. The most recommended thing I didn't see on this trip.

Temple of Karnak is back in Luxor - save it for Day 7.

**Alt:** instead of basing in Luxor and day-tripping, take a multi-day Nile cruise between Luxor and Aswan. It covers all the major riverside sites in one go (Luxor, Edfu, Kom Ombo, Aswan, usually with Abu Simbel as an add-on). I didn't do it, but it's the classic way to see this stretch.

## Day 7, Luxor

- Sunrise hot air balloon over the Valley of the Kings. Flights go at dawn. Book through your hotel or any Luxor tour operator the night before.
- Valley of the Kings: the royal tombs, including Tutankhamun's
- Temple of Hatshepsut
- Colossi of Memnon
- Felucca ride on the Nile, best at sunset
- Luxor Temple (evening, lit up at night)

Night bus to Hurghada.

## Day 8, Hurghada

Full day at the Red Sea resort. Hurghada has a different feel from Sharm - more spread out, popular with package tourists. Good for a beach day.

- Snorkelling or diving if you didn't get enough at Sharm
- Hurghada Marina for the evening - waterfront promenade lined with restaurants, cafes, and boats
- El Dahar (the old town) for a walk and cheap food
- Sahl Hasheesh for a nicer beach strip

Overnight bus back to Cairo.

## Day 9, Cairo

Arrive early morning.

- Khan el-Khalili bazaar: spices, jewellery, glassware, perfume oils. Go before noon before it gets busy.
- Egyptian Museum: mummies, the Tutankhamun collection. Allow 2 to 3 hours minimum.
- Islamic Cairo walk if you have energy: Al-Azhar Mosque, Al-Hussein area
- Last dinner somewhere in central Cairo

## Day 10, Travel out

Head to the airport in time for your flight out.

If your flight is later in the day, leave your bags at the hotel after checkout and roam Cairo a bit more before heading out.
