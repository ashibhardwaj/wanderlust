## Overview

Route: Ubud, North West Bali (Pemuteran), Nusa Penida, Kuta.

A loop through the headline parts of Bali plus a fast-boat hop to Nusa Penida. Fly into Denpasar (DPS) and base first in Ubud for the inland half: a Mt Batur sunrise trek, the jungle swings and coffee plantations, waterfalls, and temples, with a sunset run out to Tanah Lot. Then north-west to Pemuteran for scuba on a quiet stretch of coast, across to Sanur for the fast boat to Nusa Penida and its cliffs and beaches, and finally back to Kuta in the south for the beach clubs and nightlife before flying out of Denpasar.

*This ran early October, the tail of the dry season, which is the best window. The dry season runs roughly April to October; the wet season (November to March) brings afternoon downpours that make the Nusa Penida boat crossing and the dirt roads to its beaches a gamble.*

The north-west leg is the cheaper stand-in for the Gili Islands. The Gilis (and Lombok) are the classic add-on for white sand and snorkelling, but they cost more in time and money to reach. **Pemuteran**, with the **West Bali National Park** and the **Menjangan Island** reef on its doorstep, has calm, clear water for a fraction of the effort, so we based there instead and it was excellent.

### Getting around

Scooters within each town are the cheapest and easiest way around Ubud and Kuta. We self-drove a rented car for the long cross-island legs, and the fast boat covers the Nusa crossing. We drove ourselves the whole trip; **Grab** (the ride app, bikes and cars) is available and affordable if you would rather not drive. On Nusa Penida we rented a scooter too, but I would not recommend it: the terrain is steep, rough, and broken in places, so a car with a driver is the safer way to cover the island, where the sights sit at opposite ends.

*The Nusa Penida fast boats leave from **Sanur Port** (about 30 to 45 minutes across). Carriers like Maruti Express and Tanis Fast run all day, first boats around 07:30 and frequent departures into the evening; the fare was roughly INR 900 to 1000 each way when I went. Book ahead in peak season, and note these boats go to Nusa Penida itself, not Nusa Lembongan.*

### SIM and money

Pick up a SIM at the airport (Telkomsel has the widest coverage) or load an eSIM before you fly. Currency is the rupiah (IDR) and the notes carry a lot of zeros. Money changers are a known scam: some shortchange you with fast, confusing counting or sleight of hand, so use a reputable fixed-rate changer, do not go alone, count every note yourself before you leave the counter, and consider recording the exchange on your phone. Cards work in the south and bigger places, but many spots add a surcharge for paying by card, so cash is often cheaper. Carry cash anyway for Nusa Penida, the waterfalls, markets, and scooter rental.

### Vegetarian food

Vegetarian options are easy to find, but watch what they are cooked in: fish oil, shrimp paste (terasi), and fish sauce go into a lot of local dishes by default, even ones that look meat-free, so ask and confirm before ordering. Ubud and Canggu are packed with vegan and health cafes where this is a non-issue. Tempeh and tofu, gado-gado (vegetable salad with peanut sauce), nasi/mie goreng (ask for it without egg, meat, or fish), and fresh fruit and smoothie bowls are all easy wins.

### Diving and snorkelling

Our water time was off **Pemuteran** in the north-west: a scuba dive and an open-water swim in the calm, clear water of the **West Bali National Park** (Menjangan Island is the area's famous reef). If you are not certified, the Pemuteran shops run intro dives. Nusa Penida has its own well-known dive and snorkel sites (**Manta Point** for mantas year-round, **Crystal Bay**), but we spent our Nusa days on the cliffs and beaches instead.

### Spa, pools, and the Bali extras

Worth budgeting time for the things Bali is built around: a Balinese spa and massage (cheap and everywhere), a floating breakfast in the pool, an infinity pool with a view, and the Ubud swings over the jungle.

### Shopping

- **Ubud Art Market** for bamboo bags, clothes, and souvenirs (haggle)
- The Seminyak and Canggu area is the spot for clothes and boutique shopping

## Day 1, Arrival in Ubud

Land at Denpasar (DPS) in the evening and drive up to Ubud (about 1.5 hours). Check in, then dinner at a nice cafe or restaurant. A night arrival means today is just settling in; central Ubud is walkable and the evening is enough to get your bearings.

## Day 2, Ubud, the Monkey Forest, and Tanah Lot sunset

Scooter around Ubud for the day. Brunch at **Element Bali Ubud**, then the **Sacred Monkey Forest**, the forest sanctuary in the middle of town (keep sunglasses and loose items away from the monkeys). In the afternoon, head west to **Tanah Lot**, the sea temple perched on a rock offshore, for the classic Bali sunset; go before the crowd builds. Dinner at **Suku Bali** (margherita pizza and fries).

## Day 3, Mt Batur sunrise trek and central Ubud

Pre-dawn start for the **Mt Batur sunrise trek**, an active volcano with the summit reached in the dark for sunrise over the caldera and Lake Batur. The view at the top is great. Be warned that it is very commercialised: you walk up in a line with everyone else, so it is the mainstream choice rather than a quiet climb. If that is not your thing, look into the lesser-known treks instead. *Book the guided trek the day before; pickup from Ubud is around 2 AM, so plan an early night. Dress in layers, it is cold at the top and warm by the time you descend.*

Back by late morning, then an easy day around Ubud:

- **Satria Swing** and its coffee plantation, with a tasting flight of around a dozen coffees and teas; I tried them all and only skipped the luwak ("cat poop") coffee
- An afternoon by the hotel pool
- **Pura Taman Saraswati**, the lotus-pond temple in central Ubud, lovely in the evening

Dinner at **Casa Luna**, a long-running Ubud restaurant.

## Day 4, Tegenungan, Ulun Danu Beratan, and the drive north-west

Check out of Ubud and make the sights count on the way north. **Tegenungan Waterfall** first, an easy stop close to Ubud. Then up to **Ulun Danu Beratan**, the temple on Lake Bratan at Bedugul, set against the lake and hills and one of the most photographed spots on the island. From there, continue by car to **Pemuteran** on the north-west coast. *The full drive from Ubud runs long once you add the stops, so start early.*

## Day 5, Diving off Pemuteran

A water day on the quiet north-west coast: a scuba dive and an open-water swim in the calm, clear water here, the best diving of the trip. Then **Pura Pulaki**, the cliffside temple on the coast, where we caught a ceremony. An easy evening back at the resort.

## Day 6, Pemuteran to Nusa Penida

A long transit day that pays off by evening. Drive from Pemuteran across to **Sanur** (about 3.5 to 4 hours) and take the fast boat to **Nusa Penida** (30 to 45 minutes). Start early and aim for an afternoon boat so you land with daylight to spare. Check in, then **sunset at Angel's Billabong**, the natural infinity pool set in the cliffs (only safe to get into at low tide). Dinner and drinks after at the **Mambo dive resort cafe**.

## Day 7, Nusa Penida, Diamond Beach to Kelingking

The island loop, east to west, on rough roads. A car with a driver is the easier way to cover it; the terrain is hard going on a scooter. **Diamond Beach** on the east coast first, white sand under sheer cliffs with the famous stairway cut down to it, then chill through the heat of the day. Head west for **sunset at Kelingking Beach**, the T-Rex-shaped cliff and the most famous view in Bali (the climb down to the sand is steep and optional, the viewpoint alone is worth it). Evening at **Penida Colada**, a beach bar that had live Bollywood music the night we went.

## Day 8, Snorkelling, back to Bali, Kuta nightlife

Start with an early-morning snorkelling session in Nusa Penida, then take the fast boat back to Sanur and continue on to **Kuta** in the south, the beach-and-nightlife base for the last stretch. The night is for the beach clubs. The strip in nearby Seminyak is lined with them, take your pick; we went to **La Favela**, a maze of a bar done up like a Brazilian favela.

*If you want a temple or a clifftop to round out the south, **Uluwatu** (the cliff-edge temple and the Kecak fire dance at sunset) is the add-on we skipped for time.*

## Day 9, Shopping and flight out

Last morning for shopping around the south, Seminyak and Canggu for clothes and boutiques, or whatever you missed, then to Denpasar for the flight home. Kuta is close to the airport, so you do not need a big buffer for the drive.
